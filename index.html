Welcome to your digital buddy!

Congratulation on your acceptance! This application is intended to guide you through the necessary formalities which are required by almost all international students . Some of these formalities are mandatory and are also prerequisite for the other formalities.
For this reason, these formalities are listed based on their order under the formalities section of the page. Their corresponding location in Karlsruhe is indicated on a map. At each location, you can by click on the popup marker.
You can see what documents are required and their website to acquire further information about the opening hours. If you want to know how to get to a specific office double tab or double click on the marker.
This will open a window that asks you to select the location as of your Start/Go and it will give you the options to reach to that specified location by foot, car or with a bicycle. In addition to the formalities, there are University locations, studirentenwerk office, bars, restaurants and so on are included to make your first week of settlement easier.
To go to the first formality please close this window and click on the city registration button under checklist section of the page.

Formalities!

The common required formalities among foreign students are ordered by their number and this starts with the City Registration. A brief introduction and required document for each formalities can be found in the read more section.

1. City registration

This is the first thing you have to do upon arrival in Karlsruhe.

Have you register your new address in Karlsruhe?

The next step is opening/activate blocked bank accout. For this you need a Tax Identification. Additional information about Tax identification can be found on next slide.


  • Identity card(s) or Passport
  • A copy of your rental contract
  • Duly filled registration form (Anmeldeformular).

    • You can download the Anmeldeformular from the Rathaus website or get one a copy at the Rathaus itself. Confirmation of your landloard ("Formulare" "Wohnungsgeberbestätigung") the form can be found on the link below

    Link to download forms

    Everyone in Germany must register their address at the Registration Office... Einwohnermeldeamt or Burgerburo or Rathaus within the first two weeks as soon as they have found accommodation. Exceptions: if you are registered somewhere else in Germany and are staying less than 6 months in Karlsruhe or if you are from outside of Germany and only planning on living in Karlsruhe for less than 3 months. If you are still staying in a hotel but need the registration you could ask your hotel if they will act as a temporary address. You will need a letter of confirmation of your stay from the hotel to take to the Rathaus.

    2. Tax Identification number

    Have you already done your city registration?

    Great! So far, so good. The next step is getting your Tax Identification number, as it is required for opening the bank account. For this you need a Tax Identification. Additional information about opening the bank account can be found on next slide. You would normally receive the Tax ID by post two weeks after your city registration.
    If you need it urgently and still do not know where you to go, click on "GO TO MAP" below:


  • Identity card(s) or Passport
  • City registration

    • If you are not familiar you can have a brief idea why by clicking on Read More button.


    About two weeks following the registration of your place of residence in Germany, you will receive a letter from the Federal Central Tax Office.... This letter contains your tax identification number that you will need for many formal processes in Germany (e.g. receiving your salary, applying for child benefit, opening a bank account, etc.). Please keep this number safe.
    If you need to receive your tax identification number urgently and cannot wait for the letter of the Federal Central Tax Office, please contact the tax office of the city of Karlsruhe directly.
    You only need your passport or ID. You can ask for your tax identification number one workday after the registration of your new place of residence, at the earliest.

    3. Open/activate blocked bank account

    Do you already have a German bank account?

    Great! So far, so good. The next step is getting Health insurance. For this you need a bank account. Additional information about the health insurance can be found on next slide.

  • Anmeldebestätigung from the Einwohnermeldeamt
  • Identity card(s) of the person(s) to be registered (Pass / Personalausweis); for example, passport in case of Non-EU citizens.
  • Registration certificate
  • Tax Identification number
  • Passport or ID

    • Activate blocked account: For students who have already opened a blocked account... while in their home country, you should visit the bank and lift the blockage on the account. You will need the same documents as mentioned above.
    In addition, you may also need a transfer confirmation letter that you would have received when opening the blocked account in your home country.

    Some popular banks in Germany are Sparkasse, Deutsche Bank, Volksbank, Postbank, Commerzbank. Banks like N26 allow you to control your finance with a mobile app are also getting popular.

    4. Health insurance

    Do you already have health insurance?

    That's great! The next step is doing your university registration. This will be in the next slide. Both for matriculation and when applying for a residence permit (Aufenthaltserlaubnis), you will need to provide proof of sufficient health insurance coverage.


  • University admission
  • Blocked account bank statement or bank details (for direct debit)
  • Passport or ID
  • Photo
  • Minimum amount of health insurance coverage (for non-german health insurance)
  • Details of any previous German health insurance if you have one

    • Health insurance is mandatory for everyone living in Germany. If you already ... have a health insurance first please make sure the coverage is suitable for living in Germany. This can be done by asking your health insurance provider. If you dont have health insurance yet you have two possibilities; Public statutory health insurance and Private health insurance (Privat Krankenversicherung).
    In both sector there are many health insurance providers in Germany and it is your responsibility to choose and decide which insurance and benefits are right for you. We recommend taking time and reviewing the available ones carefully. For the private health insurance providers please click on the following

    5. University Registration

    Have you already registered with the university?

    That's great! You're almost there. The next step is doing either your residence permit or your public transport arrangements. This will be in the next slide. Both for matriculation and when applying for a residence permit (Aufenthaltserlaubnis), you will need to provide proof of sufficient health insurance coverage.


  • Passport or ID copy
  • University admission
  • Photo
  • Health insurance
  • Rental contract
  • Study fees

    • In order to register as a student, you will need to ... fill out the registration forms and provide the above-mentioned documents. You will then receive the campus card. By using the student ID number printed on the card, and your birth date, you will be able to use the online system to print out the Immatrikulationbescheinigung (proof of enrolment), and other documents such as KVV semester ticket coupon which you will need to redeem the KVV semester ticket (on the next step).


    6. Transport

    Do you already have your semester ticket?

    That's great! You're almost done. The next step is doing your residence permit only if you will be staying for longer than 6 months! This will be in the next slide. Choose the Transport tab to see the location.


  • Passport or ID for identification
  • Immatrikulationbescheinigung (proof of enrolment)
  • Semester ticket voucher

    • Once you have recieved the Immatrikulationbescheinigung (proof of enrolment)... you may proceed and go to the KVV office and get the semester ticket to use in trams, buses and other modes of public transport in and around Karlsruhe for 6 months. The ticket will have your details and will be given to you at no extra charge. This may be done at anytime.
    You may choose to do the residence permit first and then the ticket. For the residence permit, then proceed to the next slide for more information.


    7. Residence Permit

    Are you staying longer than 6 months in Karlsruhe?

    That's great! You're almost done. The next step is doing your residence permit only if you will be staying for longer than 6 months! Choose the Residence permit tab to see the location.


  • Passport or ID
  • A biometric passport picture
  • Approval of registration with the city
  • Certificate of Health insurance
  • Letterter of admission
  • Rental contract
  • Proof of subsistence in Germany (scholarship evidence, notarized financial certificate,
    including a certificate of sufficient financial means for the return flight; the requirement is about 853,- EUR per month)
  • Fees (for a residence permit for 12 months: approx.-100)

    • Once you have completed your university registration and/or received your ... transpotation semester ticket, you may proceed and go on to do your residence permit. This is only necessary if you are staying in Karlsruhe for more than 6 months.
    When your residence permit expires, you must apply for an extension/renewal (if necessary) well in time.
