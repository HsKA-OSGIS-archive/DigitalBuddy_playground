This application is intended to guide you through the necessary formalities which are required by almost all international students .Some of these formalities are mandatory and also are prerequisite for the other formalities. For this reason these formalities are listed based on their order under the formalities section of the page. Their corresponding location in Karlsruhe is indicated on the map. At each location by clicking on the popup marker you can see what documents are required and their website to acquire further information about their opening hours. If you also want to know how to get to a specific office double tab or double click on the marker. This will open a window that asks you to select the location as of your Start/Go and it will give you the options to reach to that specified location. In addition to the formalities layers like University locations, studirentenwerk office, bars, restaurants and so on are included to make your first week of settlement easier. To go to the first formality please closes this window and click on the city registration button under checklist section of the page.

Do you register your new address in Karlsruhe?

City registration. Everyone in Germany must register their address at the Registration Office Einwohnermeldeamt or Bürgerbüro or Rathaus within the first two weeks as soon as they have found accommodation. Exceptions: if you are registered somewhere else in Germany and are staying less than 6 months in Karlsruhe or if you are from outside of Germany and only planning on living in Karlsruhe for less than 3 months. If you are still staying in a hotel but needs the registration, you could ask your hotel if they will act as a temporary address. You will need a letter of confirmation of your stay from the hotel to take to the Rathaus.

Health insurance is mandatory for everyone living in Germany....If you already have a health insurance first please make sure the coverage is suitable for living in Germany. This can be done by asking your health insurance provider. If it is suitable then you can proceed to your next formalities which are tax, Bank account or university matriculation. by clicking next button. If you do not have health insurance yet you have two possibilities; Public statutory health insurance and Private health insurance. In both sector there are many health insurance providers in Germany and it is your responsibility to choose and decide which insurance and benefits are right for you. We recommend taking time and reviewing the available once carefully.
